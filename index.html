Who Built This

Designed by a teacher.
Not a tech bro.

Speak Easy Kids was built by Dr. Nicole Bishop — a PhD educator who has been teaching since 1996 and has spent her career figuring out how to make language stick. The program is modeled on the kind of afterschool culture clubs that already work — but redesigned around play, music, food, and games kids actually want to come back to.

No screens. No worksheets. No "repeat after me." Just an 8-week arc that runs like a story: kids meet, learn the words, use the words, and perform what they've learned for their families at the end.

How It Works

What a session looks like.

Sixty minutes, once a week, after the bell rings. Every week follows the same rhythm so kids know what to expect — but the games and themes change.

Minutes 0–10
👋

Greeting Circle

Hola. Cómo estás. Quick warm-up song. Kids check in in Spanish — even the new ones can do this by week 2.

Minutes 10–30
🎲

Theme + Game

This week's words and culture, taught through a game. Pictionary, charades, BINGO, scavenger hunts — never a worksheet.

Minutes 30–50
🎨

Make Something

Craft, song, recipe card, mini-skit. Kids leave with something physical to bring home — and a story to tell about it.

Minutes 50–60
⭐

Showcase & Send-Off

Share what we made. Stamp the "passport." Earn Speak Easy Dollars for using Spanish during class. Lined up for next week.

8-Week Curriculum

Eight themes.
Eight reasons to come back.

Each week builds on the last. By week 8, kids aren't reciting — they're performing what they learned for their families.

Week 1Hola
¡Hola!
Greetings · Names · Numbers 1–10
Name-tag craft, intro song, "find someone who…" mixer game.
Week 2Familia
Mi familia
Family · Pets · House Vocab
Family-tree drawing in Spanish, "guess who" game with family flashcards.
Week 3Comida
¡A comer!
Food · Meals · "Me gusta…"
Tasting circle with simple snacks. Order-at-the-restaurant role play.
Week 4Mercado
El mercado
Market · Money · Bargaining
Class market with Speak Easy Dollars. Kids buy/sell in Spanish.
Week 5Fiesta
¡Fiesta!
Holidays · Music · Dancing
Learn a holiday song and one folk dance from a Spanish-speaking country.
Week 6Cuentos
Había una vez
Stories · Folktales · Acting
Read a short folktale, then act it out in small groups with simple Spanish.
Week 7Juegos
¡Vamos a jugar!
Sports · Games · Verbs of Motion
Outdoor relay using Spanish commands. Pictionary tournament.
Week 8Finale
El Showcase
Family Performance · Passport Stamp
Families invited. Kids perform what they learned. Everyone takes home a passport stamped 8 times.

FAQ

The questions parents and principals actually ask.

Does my kid need to know any Spanish?
No. The whole program assumes zero Spanish on day one. By week 2, every kid can introduce themselves in Spanish.
Is this a real curriculum or just play?
Both — and that's the point. Eight written lesson plans, real vocabulary goals each week, measurable showcase outcomes. Built by a PhD educator with 30 years of classroom experience.
What does a school need to provide?
A classroom or gym, one afternoon a week, and a sign-up form. We bring the instructor, materials, games, snacks, and the finale supplies.
How many kids per session?
Sweet spot is 12–20. We can run two simultaneous groups if a school has more interest.
Can homeschool families enroll?
Yes. We run a parallel community session for homeschool families when there's enough demand. Email us with your zip code.
What if my kid misses a week?
Each lesson stands alone, but the showcase builds on what came before. We send a one-page recap so missed kids don't fall behind.
